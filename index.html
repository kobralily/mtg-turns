Turn rules and Sequencing

Magic: the Gathering turn rules breakdown for two player and commander formats

  1. Beginning Phase
  2. Main Phase 1
  3. Combat Phase
  4. Main Phase 2
  5. Ending Phase

Beginning Phase

Untap Step

This is the very first thing you do in a turn!

  • First: If there is phasing, the active player's permanents phase in or out simultaneously(t)Turn-Based Action
  • Second: If there is a day/night cycle, the day or night designation is lost and the other is gained(t)Turn-Based Action
  • Third: Any summoning sickness is lost
  • Fourth: The active player's permanents are untapped simultaneously(t)Turn-Based Action

Upkeep Step

The upkeep step doesnt have any required actions, its more like a window for you and opponents to take actions.

  • First: Abilities that are triggered in the untap or the upkeep step happen(s)Enter the Stack
  • Second: The active player gets priority

Draw Step

The active player draws a card from the top of their deck.(t)Turn-Based Action

  • First: Abilities that are triggered from drawing that card happen(s)Enter the Stack
  • Second: The active player gets priority(d)Declare Moving Phases

Declare moving to first Main Phase

This is essentially passing priority at the end of the draw step

This isn't necessarily a rule, but it's a courtesy to mention whenever you are moving to a new phase to give the opponent a chance to respond.

Precombat Main Phase

  • First: The active player puts a lore counter on each Saga Enchantment they control(t)Turn-Based Action
  • Second: Abilities that trigger "At the beginning of the next main phase" or "precombat phase" happen(s)Enter the Stack
  • Third: Once everything has happened, the active player gets priority to play any card type

At this point, in the main phase, the active player may play their one land per turn, but this is not a step. Spells and lands can be played in any order.

The main phase has no steps, and ends once the active player and opponents have finished their actions.

Declare moving to Combat

This is essentially passing priority at the end of the main phase.

Combat

Beginning of Combat

  • First: The opponent becomes the defending player. In a multiplayer game, all opponents become a "defending player"
  • Second: Abilities that trigger at the beginning of combat" happen(s)Enter the Stack
  • Third: The active player then gets priority before moving to the next step

Declare Attackers

  • First: The active player declares which creatures to attack if any(t)Turn-Based Action
  • Second: An opponent or an opponent's planeswalker must be selected for each creature's attack
  • Third: The active player then gets priority before moving to the next step

If no attackers are declared, the Declare Blockers and Combat Damage steps do not happen at all

Declare Blockers

Here, the defending player(s) will declare their blockers.

  • First: The defending player(s) choose which creatures they control will block if any(t)Turn-Based Action
  • Second: For each attacking creature that is blocked, the active player chooses the damage assignment order(t)Turn-Based Action
  • Third: Abilities that trigger off declaring blockers happen(s)Enter the Stack
  • Fourth: The active player gets priority

Combat Damage

If there is any creatures attacking or blocking with first strike or double strike, there is an extra preliminary combat damage step for the first strike damage.

  • First: Each player announces how each attacking or blocking creature they control assignes its combat damage(t)Turn-Based Action
  • Fourth: All damage is dealt simultaneously
  • Fifth: Abilities that trigger on damage will happen(s)Enter the Stack
  • Sixth: Active player gets priority

End of Combat

  • First: Abilities that trigger at the "end of combat" will happen(s)Enter the Stack
  • Second: The active player gets priority(d)Declare Moving Phases
  • Third: All creatures are removed from combat
  • Fourth: Effects that are "until end of combat" end

Declare moving to next Main Phase

This is essentially passing priority at the end of the combat phase and will alert the opponent that creatures are being removed from combat, giving the opponent a chance to respond

Postcombat Main Phase

  • First: Abilities that trigger "At the beginning of the next main phase" or "postcombat phase" are put on the stack
  • Second: Once the stack is empty, the active player gets priority to play any card type

Just like the first main phase, the active player may cast spells and play their one land per turn if they haven't yet.

The main phase has no steps, and ends once the active player and opponents have finished their actions.

Declare moving to Ending Phase

This is essentially passing priority at the end of the main phase.

Ending Phase

Beginning of End Step

  • First: Abilities that trigger at the beginning of the end step or at the next end step happen(s)Enter the Stack
  • Second: The active player gets priority

Cleanup Step

This is the last step that wraps up all the events of the turn before moving to the next player's turn.

  • First: If the active player has more cards in hand than their maximum hand size, they must discard cards from their hand until they meet the maximum
  • Second: All these actions happen simultaneously: all damage marked on permanents is removed and "until end of turn" and "this turn" effects end
  • Third: No player receives priority during the cleanup step

Now, the next player's turn begins, and the process restarts from the beginning!
